eDIS – DEALER INCENTIVE / COMMISSION SYSTEM

  1. A dealer incentive or commission is a financial inducement used by manufacturers or suppliers to motivate its dealers to sell a particular product by offering discounts on that product. Generally, this corporate sales strategy involves a reduction in the cost a dealer pays to acquire an item from a manufacturer or supplier, which increases the dealer's profit upon the sale of that item.

  2. A dealer incentive or commission may also take the form of a cash payment to a dealer for the sale of a specific item, or a cash incentive, such as a rebate, that is awarded directly to the consumer.
